Philosophy & Ritual

Months 5-8
• Focus: Philosophical Foundations & Ritual Practice

  • TsinghuaX: New Horizons in Chinese Philosophy | 中国哲学新视野 | edX

    4-5 hours/week

    Core philosophical concepts that underpin Confucian thought.

  • TsinghuaX: China’s Ancient Ritual Civilization | 中国古代礼义文明 | edX

    3-4 hours/week

    Understanding ritual practices and their significance.

  • TsinghuaX: History of Chinese Architecture | 中国建筑史 | edX (PROB DROP)

    3-4 hours/week

    Explores the historical development and cultural significance of Chinese architecture, relevant to understanding ritual spaces.

  • TsinghuaX: Pattern Studying and Making | 图案审美与创作 | edX (PROB DROP)

    2-3 hours/week

    Explores the aesthetics and creation of patterns, potentially relevant to Chinese art and ritual design.

  • Continue Chinese language practice

    2-3 hours/week

    Self-directed language practice.

    Self-Directed Study: Chinese Language Practice
    Key Activities:
    • Regular vocabulary review and expansion (focus on religious/philosophical terminology)
    • Daily listening practice with audio resources
    • Reading practice with graduated texts
    • Writing practice to reinforce character recognition
    Helpful Resources:
    • Pleco (mobile app) - Dictionary with flashcard system for Confucian terminology
    • The Chairman's Bao - Graded Chinese news articles with audio
    • Confucius Institute Online - Free language resources with cultural context
    • "Reading and Writing Chinese" by William McNaughton - Character workbook
    • "Classical Chinese for Everyone" by Bryan W. Van Norden - Specifically for classical texts

• Focus: Historical Understanding & Educational Methods

  • HarvardX: Leaders of Learning | edX

    3-4 hours/week

    2-3 hours/week

    Key Activities:
    • Focus language review on complex philosophical and historical vocabulary.

    Helpful Resources:

    • "A Source Book in Chinese Philosophy" by Wing-Tsit Chan - For text practice.

4

Sacred Texts & Application

Months 13-16
• Focus: Deep Dive into Canonical Texts & Practical Application

  • HarvardX: Religious Literacy: Scripture in Conflict | edX

    4-5 hours/week

    Critical analysis of sacred texts and their varying interpretations and applications in contemporary society.

  • HarvardX: China Humanities: The Individual in Chinese Culture | edX

    3-4 hours/week

    Provides deep humanistic and cultural context, exploring the role of the individual within Chinese society.

  • MITx: Chinese Language in Culture: Level 2 | edX

    3-4 hours/week

    Intermediate language skills for accessing more complex texts. (Optional based on progress)

  • Chinese cultural immersion through media

    2-3 hours/week

    Supplementary cultural engagement.

    Self-Directed Study: Chinese Cultural Immersion Through Media
    Key Activities:
    • Watch films/documentaries about Confucian history and culture.
    • Listen to Mandarin radio/podcasts on ethical and philosophical topics.
    • Track daily events and try to apply Confucian ethical framework.
    • Maintain a cultural journal documenting insights.
    Helpful Resources:
    • BBC's "The Story of China" (Mandarin with subtitles).
    • Chinese literature in translation, focusing on moral narratives.
    • Taiwan Public Television Service (PTS) streaming services for cultural programs.
5

Community & Ethics

Months 17-20
• Focus: Community Leadership & Ethical Frameworks

  • HarvardX: Justice | edX

    5-6 hours/week

    Develop a strong philosophical foundation in Western ethics to critically compare and contrast with Confucian moral frameworks.

  • MITx: Introduction to Western and Chinese Philosophy | edX

    3-4 hours/week

    Direct comparison of key philosophical traditions, focusing on morality and social organization.

  • HarvardX: Public Narrative: Authority and Leadership | edX

    3-4 hours/week

    Develop skills in community organizing and leadership, essential for a religious teacher's role.

  • Continue language practice (focus on classical texts)

    2-3 hours/week

    Self-directed practice to maintain and deepen access to classical sources.

    Self-Directed Study: Classical Text Practice
    Key Activities:
    • Memorize key passages from the Analects (Lun Yu).
    • Practice reading classical commentaries (Zhu Xi's annotations).
    • Attempt translation of short, non-canonical classical Chinese texts.
    Helpful Resources:
    • "A New Practical Primer of Literary Chinese" by Paul Rouzer.
    • Online Classical Chinese dictionaries (e.g., CHDICT).
    • Parallel English/Chinese editions of the Four Books.

Post-Capstone & Practical Experience

Months 25-30
• Focus: Outreach, Mentorship, & Professional Development

Phase 7 Activities (Self-Directed/Experiential)

This phase focuses on translating theoretical knowledge into practical, professional expertise.

  • Design and lead local workshops or study groups based on Confucian principles
  • Formal or informal needs assessment of a community to understand educational needs
  • Volunteer teaching opportunities to gain practical experience
  • Build relationships with established teachers for mentorship

Special Considerations for Religious Educational Path

Academic/Theoretical Focus

  • Prioritize courses providing deep philosophical and historical grounding over technical subjects (e.g., Drop Big Data/Architecture for Philosophy).
  • The most valuable courses focus on ethics, rhetoric, pedagogy, and primary texts.
  • Language acquisition is a long-term goal; the schedule integrates continuous self-study.

Practical/Vocational Focus

  • Allocate extra time for *Public Narrative* and *Rhetoric* to build core teaching/preaching skills.
  • The capstone and final phase should focus heavily on a community-facing project or internship.
  • Volunteer teaching opportunities to gain practical experience
  • Build relationships with established teachers for mentorship
